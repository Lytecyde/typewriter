We live in an action oriented society. It's been that way since we
invented agriculture and developed a stable environment in which the
tasks to be performed could be defined within. Not much thinking was
needed. We have little experience of discussing and managing subjective,
internal states - although they are as much shared experiences as
external objects visible to all. We have a general heuristic that says
we should confine our observations to the externally visible, which
kicks in to prevent the exploration of subjective phenomena even before
they have had the chance to give results and justify themselves.
